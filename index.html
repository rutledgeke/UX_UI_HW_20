    Summit Dog Rescue

    Case Study

    Our team chose the nonprofit of Summit Dog Rescue to re-design. We felt that we could help them make their site more user-friendly and enhance the design. The process started with user research through interviews and surveys to build the base of our project. We also conducted a competitive analysis. From there, we compiled the issues with design and functionality based on our research and developed a solution. Next, we started to lay out the design by wireframing and prototypes, eventually creating the final design.

    User Research

    Surveys

    We collected 28 user surveys and 5 user interviews to gather information on how people interact with animal adoption. Based on our user research we found a few interesting points. Many people go straight to the pet page and do not explore other pages on the site. We also found that over 80% of people we collected surveys from felt that the images and descriptions of the pets were most important.

    User Journey

    We found through our research that our target audience had a fairly wide range of ages. Also, our research showed that many dog owners are active and like the outdoors. With a clearer understanding of our demographics and user needs, we crafted a story to help display the needs and wants of our users. This helped us define how the user would navigate the site and what was most valuable to their experience in selecting a dog adoption agency.

    Competitive Research

    Our team was able to see that a few competitors offered some unique services that might enhance the customer experience at Summit Pet Rescue. One popular service that some other organizations offer was a food/pet supply delivery service. Our team felt that was an interesting feature.

    Problem Statement

    Summit Pet Rescue was designed to provide a platform for people to donate to the organization as well as for future pet owners to adopt rescued animals in areas local to Boulder. We have observed that our website isn't conveying information about the animals in a way that makes it easy for future owners to know what pets are currently available as well as the personalities and characteristics those pets might possess. We believe that making a website that has accurate, up to date and detailed information about the pets will lead to an increase in adoptions.
